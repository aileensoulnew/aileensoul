Most companies have completed the 2017 year-end performance discussions by now. Many people dread this yearly evaluation process, and I have certainly been one of them for quite some time. I remember a moment early in my career where I was so disappointed to have met expectations instead of having exceeded them, that I didn't sleep for a week. Let alone the thought of not meeting expectations - I couldn't imagine anything worse for my career.

My mindset towards the meaning of ratings and feedback changed when I was supporting a senior business leader in one of my previous companies to recruit an analyst for her team, and we were reviewing internal candidates together, including their development plans. One employee had received only excellent reviews over the past years, with plenty of positive remarks from her supervisor and no areas for development. The hiring manager decided to put her profile aside saying: 'if she has never received a tough message and hasn't been actively coached to improve anything in particular in years, I believe she cannot have grown to the extent that it would truly make her high performing now.' This was an eye opener that has served me well ever since.

By now I have learned to receive constructive feedback as a gift and I truly get excited about the opportunity to develop and grow by taking it to heart, while I encourage my team to do the same. Pushing through the discomfort of providing and receiving tough messages, will benefit everyone involved, and it’s the only way to drive a high performance culture in any organization.
